Welcome

Animals are all around us! They come in many shapes and sizes. Scientists group animals based on their body features and how they live.

Lets's explore the six main animal groups.

Mammals

What are mammals?

  • Mammals have hair or fur.
  • Mother mammals feed their babies with milk.
  • Most mammals give birth to live babies.
  • Most mammals live on land, but some, like whales and dolphins, live in water.

Examples

  • Lion
  • Elephant
  • Cow
  • Dog
  • Tiger
  • Monkey
  • Whale
  • Dolphin

Fun Fact

A blue whale is the largest mammal on Earth!

Birds

What are birds?

  • Birds have feathers.
  • They have beaks but no teeth.
  • They lay eggs.
  • Most birds can fly, but some, like penguins and ostriches, cannot.

Examples

  • Duck
  • Owl
  • Eagle
  • Parrot
  • Sparrow
  • Penguin
  • Ostrich

Fun Fact

The ostrich is the world's largest bird.

Fish

What are fish?

  • Fish live in water.
  • They breathe through gills.
  • They have fins to swim.
  • Most fish have scales.

Examples

  • Goldfish
  • Shark
  • Salmon
  • Tuna
  • Clownfish

Fun Fact

Sharks are fish, even though they are very different from most other fish.

Reptiles

What are reptiles?

  • Reptiles have dry, scaly skin.
  • Most reptiles lay eggs.
  • They breathe with lungs.
  • Many reptiles enjoy warm places.

Examples

  • Snake
  • Crocodile
  • Lizard
  • Turtle
  • Tortoise

Fun Fact

A turtle lives mostly in water, while a tortoise lives mainly on land.

Amphibians

What are amphibians?

  • Amphibians live both in water and on land.
  • They have smooth, moist skin.
  • Most lay eggs in water.
  • Young amphibians breathe with gills, and adults breathe with lungs and skin.

Examples

  • Frog
  • Toad
  • Salamander
  • Newt

Fun Fact

A tadpole changes into a frog as it grows.

Insects

What are insects?

  • Insects have six legs.
  • They have three body parts: head, thorax, and abdomen.
  • Most insects have one pair of antennae.
  • Many insects have wings.

Examples

  • Butterfly
  • Ant
  • Honey Bee
  • Ladybird (Ladybug)
  • Dragonfly
  • Grasshopper

Fun Fact

Butterflies taste with their feet!
