Welcome

Some animals are becoming very rare because their numbers are getting smaller. These animals are called endangered animals.

An endangered animal is an animal that is at risk of disappearing forever if it is not protected.

People around the world are working together to protect these amazing animals.

Why Do Animals Become Endangered?

Many animals become endangered because of:

🌳 Habitat Loss

When forests are cut down or wetlands are destroyed, animals lose their homes.

🚫 Illegal Hunting and Poaching

Some animals are hunted illegally for their skin, tusks, horns, or other body parts.

🌍 Pollution

Plastic, chemicals, and dirty water can harm animals and their habitats.

🌡️ Climate Change

Changes in temperature and weather make it harder for some animals to find food and safe places to live.

Some examples of Endangered Animals:-

🐅 Bengal Tiger

Where does it live?

  • Forests and grasslands

Why is it endangered?

  • Habitat loss
  • Illegal hunting

Interesting Fact

Every tiger has a unique pattern of stripes, just like human fingerprints.

🐘 Asian Elephant

Where does it live?

  • Forests and grasslands

Why is it endangered?

  • Loss of forests
  • Conflicts with humans

Interesting Fact

Asian elephants use their trunks to eat, drink, smell, and communicate.

🦏 Indian Rhinoceros

Where does it live?

  • Grasslands and wetlands

Why is it endangered?

  • Illegal hunting for its horn
  • Habitat loss

Interesting Fact

The Indian rhinoceros has thick skin that looks like natural armour.

🐼 Giant Panda

Where does it live?

  • Mountain bamboo forests

Why is it vulnerable?

  • Loss of bamboo forests

Interesting Fact

Giant pandas spend many hours each day eating bamboo.

🐢 Sea Turtle

Where does it live?

  • Oceans

Why is it endangered?

  • Plastic pollution
  • Fishing nets
  • Loss of nesting beaches

Interesting Fact

Female sea turtles return to sandy beaches to lay their eggs.

How Can We Help?

Everyone can help protect animals.

You can:

  • Protect forests and other habitats.
  • Never throw plastic or rubbish into nature.
  • Save water and keep rivers clean.
  • Plant trees.
  • Learn about wildlife and share what you know.
  • Respect wild animals and never disturb them.

Why Are Endangered Animals Important?

Every animal has an important role in nature.

If one animal disappears, it can affect many other plants and animals in the ecosystem.

Protecting endangered animals helps keep nature healthy and balanced.
