Animal Behaviour

Homepage Junior Zoologists

Welcome

Animals do many different things every day. They search for food, build homes, care for their babies, protect themselves, and communicate with other animals.

These actions are called animal behaviour.

Animal behaviour is the way an animal acts to survive, grow, and reproduce.

Feeding Behaviour

Animals eat different kinds of food depending on their needs.

Examples

  • 🦁 Lion hunts other animals for food.
  • 🐄 Cow grazes on grass.
  • 🐝 Honey bees collect nectar from flowers.

Fun Fact

Bees help plants grow by carrying pollen from one flower to another.

Defence Behaviour

Animals protect themselves from danger in different ways.

Examples

  • 🦔 Hedgehog curls into a ball.
  • 🐢 Turtle hides inside its shell.
  • 🦎 Lizard can drop its tail to escape a predator.

Fun Fact

A lizard can grow a new tail after losing one, although the new tail may not be exactly the same.

Nesting and Home Building

Many animals build homes to stay safe and raise their young.

Examples

  • 🐦 Birds build nests.
  • 🐝 Bees build hives.
  • 🐜 Ants build underground colonies.

Fun Fact

A single ant colony can contain thousands of ants.

Caring for Young

Many animals protect and care for their babies.

Examples

  • 🐘 Elephants protect their calves.
  • 🐱 Cats feed and clean their kittens.
  • 🦘 Kangaroos carry their babies in a pouch.

Fun Fact

Baby kangaroos are called joeys.

Migration

Some animals travel long distances during certain times of the year to find food or better weather.

Examples

  • 🦢 Some birds migrate to warmer places during winter.
  • 🦋 Monarch butterflies travel thousands of kilometres.
  • 🐋 Humpback whales migrate between feeding and breeding areas.

Fun Fact

Some birds return to the same nesting area every year.

Hibernation

Some animals sleep for a long time during winter when food is difficult to find.

Examples

  • 🐻 Bear
  • 🦔 Hedgehog
  • 🦇 Bat

Fun Fact

During hibernation, an animal's heartbeat and breathing become much slower.

Diurnal Animals

Diurnal animals are active during the day.

Examples

  • 🐘 Elephant
  • 🐔 Chicken
  • 🐿️ Squirrel

Fun Fact

Elephants spend up to 18 hours a day feeding because their huge bodies need a lot of food.

Nocturnal Animals

Nocturnal animals are active at night.

Examples

  • 🦉 Owl
  • 🦇 Bat
  • 🦊 Fox

Fun Fact

Owls have excellent hearing and large eyes that help them hunt in low light.

Communication

Animals communicate with each other in different ways.

Examples

  • 🐺 Wolves howl.
  • 🐬 Dolphins use clicks and whistles.
  • 🐜 Ants leave scent trails for other ants to follow.

Fun Fact

Dolphins use different sounds to communicate with each other.

Why Is Animal Behaviour Important?

Animal behaviour helps animals:

  • Find food.
  • Stay safe.
  • Build homes.
  • Care for their young.
  • Survive in different seasons.
  • Communicate with other animals.
