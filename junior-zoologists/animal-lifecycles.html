Animal Life Cycles

Homepage Junior Zoologists

Welcome

Every animal goes through a life cycle. A life cycle is the journey of an animal from birth or hatching to adulthood. As animals grow, they may change in size, shape, or appearance.

Some animals change a lot as they grow, while others look similar to their parents from birth.

Butterfly Life Cycle

A butterfly goes through four stages.

Egg

A female butterfly lays tiny eggs on the leaves of plants.

Caterpillar (Larva)

The caterpillar hatches from the egg. It eats many leaves and grows quickly.

Pupa (Chrysalis)

The caterpillar forms a chrysalis. Inside, it changes into a butterfly.

Adult Butterfly

The adult butterfly comes out of the chrysalis. It flies, feeds on nectar, and lays eggs to begin the cycle again.

Life Cycle:

Egg →Caterpillar →Chrysalis →Butterfly

Frog Life Cycle

A frog also has four main stages.

Eggs

Frogs lay their eggs in water.

Tadpole

The tadpole lives in water, breathes with gills, and has a tail.

Froglet

The froglet grows legs, develops lungs, and its tail becomes shorter.

Adult Frog

The adult frog can live on land and in water. It lays eggs to begin the cycle again.

Life Cycle:

Eggs →Tadpole →Froglet →Adult Frog

Chicken Life Cycle

Egg

A hen lays an egg.

Chick

The chick hatches from the egg and grows quickly.

Adult Chicken

The chick grows into an adult chicken. Adult hens lay eggs to continue the life cycle.

Life Cycle:

Egg →Chick →Adult Chicken

Cat Life Cycle

Kitten

A baby cat is called a kitten.

Young Cat

The kitten grows stronger and learns to hunt and play.

Adult Cat

The adult cat can have kittens of its own.

Life Cycle:

Kitten → Young Cat → Adult Cat

Elephant Life Cycle

Calf

A baby elephant is called a calf.

Young Elephant

The calf grows larger and learns from its family.

Adult Elephant

An adult elephant is fully grown and can have calves.

Life Cycle:

Calf → Young Elephant → Adult Elephant

Complete and Incomplete Metamorphosis

Some insects change their bodies as they grow.

Complete Metamorphosis

Animals pass through four stages.

Examples:

  • Butterfly
  • Beetle
  • Housefly
  • Mosquito

Stages:

Egg → Larva → Pupa → Adult

Incomplete Metamorphosis

Animals pass through three stages.

Examples:

  • Grasshopper
  • Cockroach
  • Dragonfly

Stages:

Egg → Nymph → Adult

The young nymph looks like a small adult but has no wings.

Why Are Life Cycles Important?

Life cycles help animals:

  • Grow into adults.
  • Reproduce and have young.
  • Continue their species.
  • Adapt to different stages of life.
